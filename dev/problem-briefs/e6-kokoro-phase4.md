# Problem Brief: Kokoro Phase 4 — Cosechar

## Problem Statement

After Phase 3 (Germinar), the entrepreneur has validated their model, run experiments, and launched. But launching is not arriving. Without systems for sustainable growth, measurement, and optimization, the entrepreneur falls into one of two traps: stagnation (launch worked once but doesn't compound) or chaos (growth without structure burns out the founder and the business).

Phase 4 closes the loop from "I launched" to "I have a business that grows predictably." Eduardo's methodology is explicitly sequential — the garden metaphor demands harvest after germination. Without Cosechar, the entrepreneur has a sprout but no fruit.

## Who Has This Problem

Entrepreneurs who completed Phases 1-3: they prepared the soil (strategic alignment), chose the seed (validated business model), and germinated (launched with experiments). They now have early traction — first customers, initial revenue, proof of concept — but lack the operational systems to turn traction into predictable, sustainable growth.

These are founders who need to transition from "hustling to survive" to "building a machine that works without heroics."

## Evidence

1. **Methodology completeness** — Eduardo's 4-phase framework explicitly requires all 4 phases. Phases 1-3 without Phase 4 is an incomplete coaching engagement. The parking lot (P3 priority) has tracked these skills since E1 scope.
2. **Lean Agile Marketing principle** — The methodology's fourth step is "Optimizar: mejorar y escalar con base en datos." Phase 4 skills are the concrete tools for this step.
3. **16-skill architecture** — Kokoro was designed as 16 skills across 4 phases (4 per phase). Phases 1-2 deliver 8 skills. Phase 3 adds 4. Phase 4 completes the set at 16, achieving full methodology coverage.
4. **The garden metaphor breaks without harvest** — Prepare, choose, germinate... then what? The narrative arc requires closure.

## What This Is NOT

- **Not automated growth hacking** — Eduardo coaches systems and disciplines, not shortcuts. `/kokoro-rhythm` is a weekly practice, not a dashboard hack.
- **Not generic funnel optimization** — `/kokoro-funnel` is "Funnel Consciente" — conscious, intentional, aligned with the entrepreneur's purpose and values. Not conversion rate tricks.
- **Not scaling advice** — Phase 4 is about sustainable rhythm and measurement, not "scale fast." Market for Profit, not Market Share.
- **Not a replacement for human coaching** — These skills guide the entrepreneur through Eduardo's frameworks. The entrepreneur still does the work, makes the decisions, and owns the outcomes.

## Success Criteria

1. **4 skill files delivered** — `/kokoro-factory`, `/kokoro-funnel`, `/kokoro-mafia`, `/kokoro-rhythm`, each with Eduardo's voice, methodology fidelity, and anti-pattern compliance.
2. **4 knowledge files delivered** — `kokoro-phase4-factory.md`, `kokoro-phase4-funnel.md`, `kokoro-phase4-mafia.md`, `kokoro-phase4-rhythm.md` with sufficient domain depth.
3. **Full test coverage** — Content validation tests (vocabulary, anti-patterns, structure) for all 4 skills, following the patterns established in E1 and E2.
4. **16/16 skills complete** — Kokoro methodology has full coverage across all 4 phases.
5. **Cross-phase references valid** — Phase 4 skills reference Phase 1-3 outputs correctly (e.g., `/kokoro-factory` builds on canvas from Phase 2, experiments from Phase 3).
6. **Eduardo's voice consistent** — Vocabulary substitutions enforced, anti-patterns absent, coaching tone maintained.

## Risks & Unknowns

1. **Least defined phase** — Phase 4 has the least source material in the current knowledge base. The metodologia file has only 3 lines describing Phase 4 concepts. Additional knowledge files or research into Eduardo's teachings on these topics may be required before implementation.
2. **Customer Factory Blueprint specificity** — This is a specific framework (likely Ash Maurya's). Need to understand how Eduardo adapts/teaches it, not just the generic version.
3. **"Oferta Mafia" definition** — The name suggests a "Godfather offer" (offer they can't refuse), but Eduardo's specific methodology for constructing these needs sourcing.
4. **Funnel Consciente vs. generic funnels** — Need clarity on what makes Eduardo's funnel approach distinctive enough to avoid sounding like generic marketing advice (which violates anti-patterns).
5. **Ritmo semanal scope** — "90-minute weekly rhythm + scorecard" needs specifics: what metrics, what cadence, what review format. This is operational and must feel like Eduardo, not a generic OKR check-in.
6. **Ontology dependency** — The parking lot flags ontology (P0) as foundational before E3. If E3 proceeds without ontology, Phase 4 skills will also be "isolated text" rather than nodes in a coherent knowledge graph. This risk compounds with each phase.
