# Luxelling Assessment — Positioning Framework

> Classify an invitado's business into luxury, premium, or standard positioning
> using the Triangulo Funcional-Simbolico-Emocional (F-S-E) framework.

## The Triangulo F-S-E

The core differentiator between positioning tiers is NOT the price — it is
the **justification** of the price.

| Dimension | What it measures | Premium signal | Luxury signal |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Funcional (F)** | Desempeno, utilidad, conveniencia | High — features drive value | Low — utility is assumed, not sold |
| **Simbolico (S)** | Diseno, origen, herencia, estatus | Low-Medium | High — meaning drives value |
| **Emocional (E)** | Experiencias, sensaciones, pertenencia | Medium | High — emotional resonance |

**Direction rule:**
- F-driven upward = Premium positioning
- S/E-driven upward = Luxury positioning

## Positioning Map — Three Universes

| Axis | Mass Market | Premium | Luxury |
|------|------------|---------|--------|
| Core focus | Needs / pain | Superior functionality | Desire, status, pleasure |
| Business objective | Volume + penetration | Functional differentiation + margin | Exceptional value + pricing power |
| Distribution | Mass (hyper-capillarity) | Selective | Hyper-selective / boutiques |
| Desired penetration | Maximum | High in profitable niches | Intentionally limited |
| Operations | Hyper-efficiency, outsourcing | Mixed | Vertical integration, craftsmanship |
| Client experience | Speed + availability | Solid service | Ritual + 360 emotion |
| Price vs mass | Competitive (market average) | +20-100% vs mass | 4-20x mass, status signal |
| Key metrics | Units / share / rotation | Margin, ROIC, NPS | Resale value, wait list |

## Assessment Questions (5-question version for skills)

These 5 questions determine positioning tier. Score each 0 or 1.

| # | Question | 0 = Standard/Premium | 1 = Luxury |
|---|----------|---------------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Value type:** Is your differentiation primarily symbolic/emotional rather than functional? | Functional features drive purchase | Meaning, status, emotion drive purchase |
| 2 | **Distribution:** Do you control access and limit distribution intentionally? | Available broadly or selectively | Hyper-selective, invitational access |
| 3 | **Price point:** Is your investment 4x+ above the mass-market equivalent? | 1-3x above mass | 4-20x above mass |
| 4 | **Production control:** Do you maintain vertical integration or artisanal processes? | Outsourced or mixed | Own production, craftsmanship, artisanal |
| 5 | **Buyer motivation:** Do your invitados buy for what it REPRESENTS rather than what it DOES? | Buy for utility/performance | Buy for belonging, identity, status |

### Scoring Interpretation

| Score | Tier | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 4-5 | **Luxury** | Strong luxury positioning — luxury skills fully applicable |
| 2-3 | **Premium** | Premium with luxury potential — selective luxury principles apply |
| 0-1 | **Standard** | Mass-market or early premium — focus on base Kokoro methodology first |

## Extended Diagnostic (10-question version)

For deeper assessment, use the full 10-question matrix (score 1-5 each):

1. **Functionality:** Is your differentiation technical/functional?
2. **Symbolism:** Does your value reside in meaning/status?
3. **Emotion:** Do you generate memorable experiences?
4. **Exclusivity:** Do you control access and distribution?
5. **Experience:** Is every touchpoint ceremonial?
6. **Price:** Are you 4-20x above mass market?
7. **Access:** Is your purchase process complex/invitational?
8. **Production:** Do you maintain vertical control?
9. **Service:** Do you transform (not just satisfy)?
10. **Timelessness:** Is your creation atemporal?

**Interpretation:**
- 35-50: Consolidated luxury
- 25-34: Premium-Luxury territory
- 15-24: Established premium
- below 15: Mass market or transition

## Dynamic Processes

### Luxurization (Premium to Luxury)
- Add storytelling and heritage
- Create memorable experiences
- Limit access and distribution
- Develop artistic elements
- **Indicators:** Higher margin, wait lists, resale value

### Commoditization (Luxury/Premium to Mass) — AVOID
- Loss of perceived value
- Price wars, frequent discounts
- Massification, loss of exclusivity

## Anti-patterns

- Do NOT classify based on price alone — a high price without symbolic/emotional
  justification is overpriced premium, not luxury
- Do NOT assume B2B cannot be luxury — professional services, consulting, and
  experiences can absolutely position as luxury
- Do NOT skip the assessment to jump straight to luxury skills — the tier must
  be earned through evidence, not aspiration
- Do NOT treat the assessment as a gate that blocks — it is a compass that guides.
  Even standard-tier invitados benefit from understanding their position
