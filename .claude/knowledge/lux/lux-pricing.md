# Lux by Kokoro Pricing — Estrategia de Precios en Lujo

> In luxury, pricing is philosophy, not mathematics. The price creates the
> brand, not the other way around.

## Core Principle

> "Un buen negocio sano camina DESDE la rentabilidad, no HACIA la rentabilidad."

A price that doesn't transmit value creates confusion. Selling a luxury creation
at a non-luxury price generates **incoherence** that will be penalized by the
market. Price is the easiest thing to change but has the most durable impact
on value perception.

## The Veblen Effect

**Definition:** When price increases **elevate desirability** and increase demand.
Counterintuitive — violates classical price-demand elasticity.

### Case: Chanel During the Pandemic (2021)
- While most brands discounted to clear stock, Chanel **raised prices** for the 4th time
- Result: Demand **skyrocketed** — buyers lined up from dawn for $9,500 bags
- Five months later: 5th price increase
- **Why:** Buyers expected Chanel to raise prices, not lower them

### Case: Patek Philippe Nautilus 5711
- Discontinued their best-selling $33,000 watch
- All inventory sold next day
- Secondary market: **$150,000-$200,000**
- Future relaunch estimated at $80k-$90k — will seem "affordable" vs $200k secondary

## Four Pricing Strategies

| Strategy | Method | Luxury fit |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **Cost Plus** | Cost + desired margin | NOT recommended — focuses on cost, not value |
| **Competitive** | Based on direct competition | Limited — luxury is cross-categorical |
| **Dynamic** | Real-time demand-based | With caution — arbitrariness can damage brand |
| **Value-Based** | Based on perceived value delivered | RECOMMENDED — "not how much, but how I explain the value" |

## The 3 Principles of Luxury Pricing

### Principle 1: Never Use Cost Plus
Don't price by artisan hours or square meters. Price by **intangible attributes**
and symbolic value. For new products, reference the most basic product of an
established luxury brand in your category.

### Principle 2: Luxury ALWAYS Increases Prices Over Time
- **Mass market:** Launch high → progressively lower to capture more market
- **Luxury:** Set legitimizing price → **systematically increase**
- Creates investment dynamic: "If I don't buy today, tomorrow it's more expensive"
- Triggers Veblen Effect: price increase → desirability increase

### Principle 3: Luxury Does NOT Discount
Discount is the **second kryptonite** (first is massification).

**Implicit message of discount:** "Not even I believe the value I communicate."

If you explain value coherently and then discount, you contradict your
proposition. Better: price adjusted to value from the start.

## Framework: The 3 Variables

### Variable 1: Consumer Budget (How irrational is the purchase?)
- For your target, what economic effort does your price represent?
- **Main reason to buy:** Desire (more desire → purchase feels more assumable)
- **Main reason NOT to buy:** Fear of making a mistake (not lack of desire)
- **Strategy:** Activate desire first, then remove fear (remind of high resale value)

### Variable 2: Comparison vs. Mass Market
- Must be **significantly above** mass market equivalents
- Acceptable ranges: 2x, 5x, 10x up to 200x above mass
- Being at the same level or slightly above = loss of legitimacy

### Variable 3: Comparison vs. Existing Luxury
- Reference: similar to the **most basic** product of established luxury in your category
- This is the starting point — can evolve upward

## Value Justification: 5 Key Attributes

1. **Mistica de marca** — Authentic stories, legends, myths (La Prairie, Hermes)
2. **Reputacion** — Consistency in proclaimed values; buyers identify with these
3. **Calidad + servicios exclusivos** — Extended product: the entire experience
4. **Propuestas unicas / escasez** — Limited access, exclusive collaborations
5. **Hiperpersonalizacion** — Unique AND personalized (Nuwa luxury travel: no fixed rates)

## Communication of Price

### Seduction Before Price
Price is communicated **AT THE END**. First: complete transmission of value.
A good luxury salesperson is a great conversationalist, listener, and seducer.

### Price Opacity
- In physical stores: prices **not evident**
- No large tags — available only when the salesperson reveals them
- On web: explain value before showing price

### Round Prices, NOT Odd
- **Avoid:** $17.95, $24.95 (image of cheap)
- **Use:** Very round numbers
- Without currency symbol when possible (reduces perception of "sacrifice")

### Anchoring Technique: "The Most Expensive in the World"
- Turron 1880: "The most expensive turron in the world" (generates expectation)
- The actual price then seems "reasonable"

## Promotion Rules (When Unavoidable)

### General Principle: Gift > Discount
- **Better:** Elevate value (gift additional products)
- **Worse:** Admit value wasn't real (discount price)

### 2 Conditions for Promoting
1. **Reward loyal invitados** — Systematic buyers, not occasional "date trippers"
2. **New invitados with loyalty potential** — Must have potential to become regulars

### 4 Emergency Promotion Rules
1. **NEVER penalize quality/service standards** — Client remembers betrayal forever
2. **Maintain standards for new invitados** — First impression must be impeccable
3. **Have temporal excuse** — Holiday, anniversary, limited duration
4. **Even promotional buyers must have full-price potential**

## Diagnostic Questions

| Question | Red Flag |
|----------|----------|
| Does your price reflect delivered value? | Frequent price negotiation |
| Is it significantly above mass market? | Comparison with mass by invitados |
| Is it coherent with existing luxury in category? | Need for discounts to close |
| Does value communication precede price? | High rotation of unsold inventory |

## Anti-patterns

- **Cost-plus thinking** — "My materials cost X, so I charge X + margin"
- **Competitive pricing** — "My competitor charges Y, so I charge Y - 10%"
- **Discounting to move inventory** — Destroys years of brand building overnight
- **Showing price before value** — Price without context is always "too expensive"
- **Odd pricing** — $199.99 screams mass market, not luxury
